Audit Finding 2

Consolidation Candidates

10 groups of pages can be merged into stronger pillars. Each redirect concentrates link equity, reduces crawl waste and creates one authoritative URL instead of several thin ones.

Wine Country Pillar3 REDIRECTS
  • KEEP /wine-country/ — expand into pillar
  • REDIRECT /wineries-and-tasting-rooms/
  • REDIRECT /wine-events/
  • REDIRECT /wine-tours/
Why: Three sub-pages (65–114 words) serve the same searcher intent as the parent.
LoCo Ale Trail Pillar2 REDIRECTS
  • KEEP /loco-ale-trail/ — expand into pillar
  • REDIRECT /breweries-and-tasting-rooms/
  • REDIRECT /brewery-events/
  • KEEP /loco-ale-trail-passport/ — conversion page
Why: Breweries and events sub-pages repeat the parent. Passport stays — unique conversion intent.

  • REDIRECT /equestrian-event-facilities/
  • REDIRECT /horseback-riding/

Trails & Parks1 REDIRECT
  • KEEP /hiking-and-trails/ — absorb parks content
  • REDIRECT /parks-and-gardens/

Why: Near-empty page. Fold B&B names into the parent accessibility page.
Net result: 19 thin pages redirected into 9 stronger pillar URLs. Crawl index reduced from 73 to approximately 54 main-navigation pages.

Pages that exist but have clearly weak content relative to their search demand. The bar charts show current word count vs. the target. These need expansion, not deletion.

For the Room

Questions for Discussion

These are the decisions I'd like the team's input on. I have recommendations for each, but I want to hear how these align with what everyone else is working on.

Which town pages should we tackle first?

Ashburn (73K impressions) and Leesburg (county seat, declining clicks) — but the strat plan also calls out Middleburg and eastern Loudoun. What does the team think?

Which holiday do we pilot first?

Fall Season is highest volume. Valentine's Day gives us the longest runway. Mother's Day has an existing blog post we can redirect. Which is the best pilot?

What should the Locals page look like?

The strat plan calls for community involvement and local storytelling. CTA-curated itineraries, resident-submitted recommendations, a mix? Who owns the content?

Can we redirect the 19 thin pages this month?

Quick wins that require CMS changes but no editorial work. Can we get these done in the next two weeks?

Who owns the annual holiday refresh cycle?

Each hub needs a refresh 6–8 weeks before the holiday. Should this be a recurring Asana task? Who's responsible for the annual update?

What role should the agency play?

Miles Partnership has been recommending many of these structural changes since September 2025. Should we loop them in on implementation, or handle internally first?
